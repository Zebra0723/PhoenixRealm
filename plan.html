Good to know

Frequently asked questions

A few things visitors often ask before they arrive.

What does a ticket include?

One ticket gives you entry to all three realms — Mythos Family Realm, HydroRealm and Adolescent Abyss — for the full day.

Which realm is right for my group?

Mythos Family Realm suits families and younger visitors, HydroRealm is built around water rides for all ages, and Adolescent Abyss is the thrill-focused realm for guests aged 12 and over.

Are there age restrictions on the rides?

The high-energy rides in Adolescent Abyss are designed for guests aged 12+. Mythos Family Realm and HydroRealm welcome all ages, with gentler attractions for younger visitors.

Where can we eat?

Food outlets are spread throughout the park, from quick stands to a sit-down restaurant and cafe, so you can find something without leaving the realm you're in.

Are toilets and first aid easy to find?

Yes. Each realm has its own toilet block and first aid block, with benches and bins placed across the park for convenience.

When is Phoenix Realm open?

Phoenix Realm is open daily, all year round. Opening hours can vary on holidays, so check before you travel.

Why are some tickets sold out on certain dates?

Weekends and school holidays are our busiest times, so availability can be limited. Weekdays during term time usually have the most tickets available.

Accessibility

Access for everyone

We want every guest to enjoy their day. Here's what's in place across the park, and our team is always happy to help with anything else.

Step-free access

Most paths and attractions across the three realms are step-free, with ramps and gentle gradients where the ground changes level.

Accessible toilets

An accessible toilet is available in every realm, alongside the standard toilet blocks, so facilities are always close by.

Wheelchair & scooter hire

A limited number of wheelchairs and mobility scooters can be hired from the main entrance. We recommend reserving ahead on busy days.

Ride access guide

Each ride lists its access requirements at the entrance, and staff can talk you through transfers and any restrictions before you board.

Quieter times

Weekday mornings are usually the calmest. Guests who prefer fewer crowds and lower noise may find these the most comfortable times to visit.

Carers & assistance dogs

Essential carers can visit free of charge through our carer ticket scheme, and assistance dogs are welcome throughout the park.
